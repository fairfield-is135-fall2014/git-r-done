Fairfield University Business Plan Competition

Getting Started

  1. Register on the website. When registering we highly recommend using your LinkedIN, Google+, or Facebook account to speed up the process. After logging in the first time, you will be asked a few questions about yourself, starting with general questions like your name, etc., and then more specific questions about your class, major, and skills & experience. You will not be registered to compete until your profile is complete. If your registration gets interrupted, you can resume it by logging in again by the same method you used before. The system will then prompt you to complete your registration.

  2. Confirm your eligibility to win. The eligibility rules are given in the Rules & Policies section of the Competitors Guide. Please free to contact us to discuss any questions about your eligibility.

    3. Eligibility Requirements

    1. All currently-enrolled Fairfield University students are eligible to participate unless they or their team(s) violate one of the other conditions below. Non-students and former students are not eligible to compete.
    2. In order to win a prize, each student competitor must ...
      • Be currently enrolled at Fairfield University and in good standing at the University and the community
      • Complete the full registration process on this website. If your profile does not appear in the Directory, then your registration is incomplete.
      • Allow the Business Plan Competition to store copies of submitted business models, plans, and other work for judging and archival purposes. The Business Plan Competition will not distribute copies of any plan without the permission of the authors.
    3. In order to win prize money at the Finale, each team must ...
      • Register as a team on the website. If your team is not in the Teams Directory then you are not eligible to win.
      • Maintain a team roster of registered competitiors. Rosters must have between 1 and 4 student members.
      • Acquire and then work with mentors (up to 2) as required.Teams without a registered mentor listed on their team rosters by the designated date will not be permitted to continue. Further, teams will be required to provide evidence that they have actively worked with their mentors throughout the authoring and revision process.
      • Sign a media waiver permitting Fairfield University to use photographs, video, and audio for educational, research, and promotional purposes.
      • Complete all required activities, including public pitch presentations that include information about your business ideas and plans.
    4. Submitted business models and business plans must ...
      • Propose a new seed or startup venture. Proposals for tax shelter opportunities, real estate syndications, existing firms with a new product introduction, and industry analyses are not eligible.
      • Be the original work of the team. The author(s) will retain all rights regarding the use of all submitted business models and plans at all times prior to, during, and following the competition.

  3. Polish up your public directory profile. Once registered, you can take your time spiffing up your public profile that will be published in the competition directory. Log in using whatever method (LinkedIN, Google+, Facebook, username/password) you used to register. (Note: this is very important; if you choose another method then you may end up with two accounts and lots of confusion later on.) Then you can change your picture, craft your skills & experience entry, and even add social links to your profile listing.

  4. Ask lots of questions. There is a lot of material here on the website, which can make things confusing sometimes, especially in the beginning. So speak up when that happens! Your questions will help us make it better for you and everyone who comes after you. If you don't know who to ask, use our Contact Us page to email your question, and we'll get it to the right person.
